5 Traditions of Focus

Philosophy of Hinduism: Brahman, Atman, and Sanatana Dharma

Hinduism explores the cosmic order through concepts of action and cosmic consequence (Karma) and cyclical migration of the soul (Samsara). It introduces non-dual metaphysics (Advaita) asserting the essential unity of the individual soul (Atman) and the ultimate universal reality (Brahman).

Brahman Atman Samsara Moksha

Buddhism: The Diagnosis of Suffering and the Eightfold Path

Buddhism offers a diagnostic approach to human existence. It starts with the realization of suffering (Dukkha) and impermanence (Anicca) before recommending self-mastery through the Middle Way and the Noble Eightfold Path to achieve Nirvana.

Four Noble Truths Eightfold Path Impermanence Nirvana

Judaism: Covenantal Law, Ethics, and Tikkun Olam

A monotheistic covenantal tradition grounded in sacred history and Torah law. Judaism emphasizes moral responsibility, justice, and the active human duty to participate in the restoration of cosmic order (Tikkun Olam).

Torah Tikkun Olam Shabbat Talmud

Christianity: Incarnation, Radical Love, and Forgiveness

Originating from 1st-century Judaism, Christianity centers on the life, crucifixion, and resurrection of Jesus. Core tenets emphasize the Incarnation of God, the restorative capacity of Grace, and the moral duty of loving enemies.

Incarnation Grace Beatitudes Atonement

Islam: Absolute Divine Unity, Tawhid, and the Five Pillars

Grounding its metaphysics in absolute divine oneness (Tawhid), Islam posits direct submission to God's will. The practice framework is detailed inside the Five Pillars, establishing individual, social, and economic equity.

Tawhid Five Pillars Nubuwwah Quran
