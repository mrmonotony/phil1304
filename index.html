5 Traditions of Focus

Philosophy of Hinduism: Brahman, Atman, and Sanatana Dharma

Hinduism explores cosmic order through the dual actions of Karma and cyclical transmigration (Samsara). Standard exams measure precise vocabulary comprehension of the identical nature of Atman (individual self) and Brahman (ultimate cosmic source).

Brahman & Atman Samsara & Karma Moksha Liberation The Upanishads

Buddhism: The Diagnosis of Suffering & The Eightfold Path

Offers a therapeutic and philosophical framework addressing the reality of suffering (Dukkha), the impermanence of the physical and psychological worlds (Anicca), and the realization of non-self (Anatta) culminating in Nirvana.

Four Noble Truths Eightfold Path Impermanence (Anicca) Nirvana Resolution

Judaism: Covenantal Law, Ethics, and Tikkun Olam

Grounded in covenantal relationships and sacred histories, Judaism prioritizes ethical execution and community restoration (Tikkun Olam). Mastery of Torah narratives and legal debates within the Talmud are central.

Torah Covenant Tikkun Olam Shabbat & Sacrality Talmudic Analysis

Christianity: Incarnation, Radical Love, and Forgiveness

Derived from first-century Judeo-messianic movements, Christianity centers upon the Incarnation of God, the redemptive capacity of Grace, and radical ethics of forgiveness established in the Beatitudes.

Incarnation Theology Saving Grace Beatitudes & Love Atonement models

Islam: Absolute Divine Oneness (Tawhid) and The Five Pillars

Islam posits direct submission to God's will on the foundation of absolute divine oneness (Tawhid). Doctrinal evaluation covers the life of Prophet Muhammad and the behavioral mandates of the Five Pillars.

Tawhid Oneness Five Pillars Prophethood (Nubuwwah) The Holy Quran
